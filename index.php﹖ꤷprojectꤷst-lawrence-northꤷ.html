St. Lawrence North

Type:
Exhibition

Project:
New Museum for the History of Toronto

Location:
The Esplanade, Toronto ON

Site:
2,600m2

Program:
Exhibition Space 2,300m2; Office 560m2; Auditorium 450m2; Food Court 400m2; Multi-Purpose Hall 800m2

...................................................

St. Lawrence North is a 6500m2 multi-use facility in downtown Torontoâ€™s historic core. The primary function of the building is display space promoting Toronto culture, with new public spaces and expanded market areas.

The design was developed to preserve the character of the neighborhood and still deliver a contemporary design with dynamic spaces. A continuous skin around the administrative program creates a tension that juxtaposes the new and the existing context. Primary circulation leads visitors through each level of exhibit space. These corridors are oriented to create unique visual connections with the city, including Old City Hall, St. James Park and the nearby Financial District. St. Lawrence North effectively becomes a lens through which the city is revealed.
